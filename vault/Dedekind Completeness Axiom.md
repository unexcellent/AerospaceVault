Defines the difference between $\mathbb{Q}$ and $\mathbb{R}$.

If $\mathbb{R} = L \cup R$, such that $L \neq \emptyset, R \neq \emptyset$ and such that $\forall x \in L, y \in R: x < y$, then $\exists ! s \in \mathbb{R}: x \le s \le y$.
